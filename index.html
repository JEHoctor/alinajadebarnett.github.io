I am an assistant professor of statistical science at Duke University. I frequently find myself studying fundamental properties of different statistical concepts and am broadly interested in network analysis, causal inference and computational constraints of statistical methods. I am the Associate Director of the new Polarization Lab at Duke.

I am one of the organizers of the SAMSI Semester Program on Causal Inference in Spring 2020. The ppening workshop will be in held in December 2019. More details to follow!

I help run the New Researchers Group at the Institute of Mathematical Statistics which coordinates the Meeting of New Researchers in Statistics and Probability. Contact me if you would like to get involved and join us next year in Colorado.

I will also be attending the following meetings with various talks and symposia: AISTATS 2019, ACIC 2019, JSM 2019, ...
